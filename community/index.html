PATTAYA VILLA STREAM runs 6 to 8 hours every night — and the people who keep that possible aren't Tim and Paemi alone. Mods hold chat together. Supporters fuel the show through YouTube. Regulars answer the same questions every night so new viewers feel at home. This page explains how that works.

// 01 — VOLUNTEERS

THE MODS.

A small team of volunteer chat moderators. They give the show structure without ever asking for a stage. Unpaid, on shift most nights, the reason the chat doesn't melt down at 1 AM.

If you're being a problem in chat, you'll hear from one of them before you hear from us. Full enforcement rules live on the chat code of conduct — one warning, then ban for most offenses; instant ban for spam and slurs.

// FREE SUPPORT COUNTS

Money or no money — you matter.

On the support page we split paths into free and paid — Subscribe, Watch, Share versus Super Chat and Super Thanks. Both sides feed the same engine. Subscribes and watch time tell YouTube the villa stream is worth recommending. Super Chat adds direct support on top.

The support page says it plainly: every viewer matters, money or no money. You don't need to pay to be part of the community. Showing up nightly, saying hi in chat, sharing the link — that's real support.

Free support is real support. Paid support gets a live shoutout. Both keep the show running sponsor-free.
★ HOW TO BECOME A REGULAR

Three steps. No badge required.

  1. Show up. Watch live, every night you can. Even 20 minutes counts. The algorithm sees you, and so does the chat.
  2. Be in chat. Say hi when you arrive. Reply to other viewers. Drop the kind of comments you'd want to read yourself. The regulars are just people who kept showing up and being human.
  3. Don't try. There's no “regular” application. No badge to earn. After enough nights, the room knows you. That's the only step that matters.

// MOD INVITES

Mods are invited. Never applied for.

The FAQ and chat code say the same thing: be active in chat for 30+ nights, stay respectful, become known to the regulars — and we approach you. Don't DM Tim, Paemi, or anyone asking to mod. That disqualifies you.

Mods enforce the code, remove spam, and keep a six-to-eight-hour broadcast readable. They don't speak for the show and they don't get listed here. If you want to help the room, the best path is simple: be a good regular first.
